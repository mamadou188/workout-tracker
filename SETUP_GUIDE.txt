# Workout Tracker — GitHub Pages Setup Guide

Follow these steps once. After that, your app lives at a permanent HTTPS URL,
works fully offline, and your data persists forever in the browser's localStorage
(never evicted, because the app has a real origin).

---

## Step 1 — Create a free GitHub account (skip if you have one)

Go to https://github.com and sign up. It's free.

---

## Step 2 — Create a new repository

1. Click the "+" icon → "New repository"
2. Name it exactly:  workout-tracker
3. Set it to **Public**
4. Do NOT tick "Add a README"
5. Click **Create repository**

---

## Step 3 — Upload the files

On the empty repository page, click **"uploading an existing file"** (or drag-and-drop).

Upload ALL of these files (they are in the ZIP you downloaded):
  - index.html
  - sw.js
  - manifest.json
  - icon-192.png
  - icon-512.png

Click **Commit changes**.

---

## Step 4 — Enable GitHub Pages

1. Go to your repository → **Settings** tab
2. Scroll to **Pages** in the left sidebar
3. Under "Branch", select **main** and folder **/ (root)**
4. Click **Save**

GitHub will show: "Your site is live at https://YOUR-USERNAME.github.io/workout-tracker/"

This takes about 1–2 minutes the first time.

---

## Step 5 — Add to iPhone Home Screen

1. Open Safari on your iPhone
2. Go to:  https://YOUR-USERNAME.github.io/workout-tracker/
3. Tap the **Share** button (box with arrow pointing up)
4. Tap **"Add to Home Screen"**
5. Tap **Add**

The app icon will appear on your home screen. Open it — it will look and feel
like a native app (no browser UI, full screen).

---

## How data persistence works now

- Your workout data is stored in the browser's localStorage for that URL
- Because it has a real HTTPS origin (github.io), Safari's 7-day eviction
  policy does NOT apply — data persists indefinitely
- The Service Worker (sw.js) caches the app so it loads instantly even with
  no internet connection
- Your data lives on your iPhone. It does not go to any server.

---

## Updating the app in future

If you get an updated index.html:
1. Go to your GitHub repository
2. Click on index.html → click the pencil icon (Edit) → or drag the new file
3. Commit — the live site updates within minutes
4. On your iPhone, open the app once with internet so the Service Worker
   picks up the new version

---

## Backup recommendation

Even though data now persists reliably, it's still on one device.
Use the app's built-in **Backup** button regularly and save the text
somewhere (Notes app, email to yourself, etc.) to guard against
phone loss or factory reset.
